AI GeoGuessr Hacker | Chrome Extension

Motivation:

  • Last fall break, my friends and I decided to play GeoGuessr: an online geography game where players guess locations around the world based on Google Street View images.
  • My friends had spent years practicing, and knew every "meta" in the game. Unsurprisingly, I was only able to win a few games out of the countless matches we played. I realized I needed every advantage I could get...

Solution:

  • Naturally, I developed a chrome extension to help level the playing field.

    • How it works:

    • Once the user inputs their OpenAI api key and clicks the "capture" button, a request to OpenAI's Vision endpoint is made with the base64 encoded image and prompting asking for the exact estimated coordinates.
    • Next, the coordinates are parsed from the returned JSON and passed into an interactive Google Maps iFrame, which highlights the estimated coordinates with a pin.
    • This whole process takes around 5 seconds, leaving the user ample time to input their GeoGuessr answer even in the fastest gamemode.
  • This ended up being a super fun way to learn how to develop, package, and publish a chrome extension, design a store page, and prank my friends! I hope to add many more updates in the future.
  • As of now, (a few weeks after releasing) the Chrome Extension has over 1,000 downloads and 500 weekly active users.
  • Check it out yourself here if you're interested.

Tfrrs Scratch Prediction Algorithm | Java Program

Motivation:

  • Last spring, I realized that all of my teammates were spending hours trying to manually deduce who would scratch (not compete) from the First Rounds of the NCAA Championships. I figured there had to be a better way!

Solution:
